About Me

I am a recent graduate in the robotics Masters program at Oregon State University. I have experience in hardware development, programming, system integration, robotics, and project management. I am interested in the interaction and collaboration between complex robotic systems and human users. Specifically, how specific robot behaviors and interactions can be leveraged to elicit expected responses from users to achieve better overall performance and comfort with the system.

Education and Experience

I graduated Magna Cum Laude with an Honors Bachelor of Science degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Oregon State University in Fall of 2017, with a 3.84 GPA and OSU Undergraduate Research Fellow transcript notation.

I completed a Master of Science in Robotics at Oregon State University in the spring of 2019. During this time, I was a graduate researcher in the Personal Robotics Group under William D. Smart. For my graduate thesis, I developed and performed a human-robot interaction study which explored possible differences in the way humans comply with instructions given by a person using a telepresence robot and a person wearing Ebola-style personal protective equipment. This was a first step in investigating the use of telepresence robots for infectious disease outbreaks in order to limit the spread of further infection. Robotics at OSU is a growing program with a multitude of research areas, check out the CoRIS institute for more information at robotics.oregonstate.edu.
